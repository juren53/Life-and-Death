 
 
There is a whole post-crash story about how my Dad found out later that evening that Hennessy flew with his buddy Jim Bissell in the war but Mom was so
traumaetized by the crash that she did not want it discussed in the house or around the dinner table.  In the days following, Dad would whisper bits and
pieces to us as we pumped him for information.   To me it was inconceivable that the man who lost his life in the crash down the street know Mr. Bissell.

Dad was at work that Saturday, and I think he might have called Jim from work.  Dad tried give us the news over the dinner table but Mom shut down the conversation.  The Bissells lived in Glendale about 20 minutes away.  Jim Bissell flew corporate jets for Disney out of Burbank so its conceivable he and Jack Hennessy stayed in contact as war buddies often do.  My folks and the Bissells were part of a 'supper club' that met several times a year sampling the variety of restaurants in the LA area.  I never spoke directly with Jim Bissell about Hennessy or his war experiences.  We moved to St. Louis in 1968 and Christmas cards were the extent of our communication after that.
