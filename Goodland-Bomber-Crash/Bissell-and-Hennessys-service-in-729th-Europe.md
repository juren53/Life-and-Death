## 📊 The Numbers: 452nd Bomb Group

The 729th was one of four squadrons that made up the 452nd. Across the entire group, the losses were heavy:

* **Aircraft Lost:** The 452nd lost approximately **110 B-17s** in combat over its 250 missions.
* **Personnel Lost:** Over **750 men** from the 452nd were shot down.
* **Survival Breakdown:**
* **POWs:** Hundreds were captured and held in German camps like Stalag Luft IV.
* **Evaders:** About **61 men** successfully evaded capture with the help of the French or Dutch resistance.
* **Neutral Countries:** **74 men** managed to land or crash in neutral territory (like Sweden or Switzerland) and were interned.



### ⚠️ A Dangerous Era

The 452nd gained a reputation for facing brutal opposition early in its tour. For example:

### The "Killed vs. Captured" Ratio

In the Eighth Air Force, for every man wounded, roughly three were killed. This was because if a B-17 exploded or folded under flak, the centrifugal forces often prevented the crew from reaching the hatches. In the 729th, a "bad day" usually meant the entire 10-man crew was lost at once.
